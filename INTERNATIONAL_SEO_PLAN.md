# International SEO Plan

## Current baseline
- Default landing locale: `en`
- Alternate locales: `th`, `zh`
- `x-default`: English landing pages
- Public sitemap coverage: home, crypto, cash, legal, exchange detail, money changer detail
- Structured data: `Organization`, `WebSite`, `BreadcrumbList`

## Why English is the primary landing layer
- English is the broadest common search language for travelers, expats, and international users looking for Thai baht routes.
- It gives the site one clean default for `x-default`, canonical messaging, and future country-specific expansion.
- It reduces the risk of thin translated pages being indexed before the core landing layer is stable.

## Target search intent clusters

### Core comparison intent
- `crypto to THB`
- `BTC to THB Thailand`
- `USDT to THB`
- `cash to THB Bangkok`
- `money changer Bangkok`

### Traveler / expat intent
- `best way to get Thai baht`
- `exchange cash in Bangkok`
- `Bangkok money changer near Pratunam`
- `Thailand crypto exchange for THB`

### Future international intent
- Japan: `JPY to THB Bangkok`, `Japan to Thailand exchange rate`
- Korea: `KRW to THB Bangkok`, `Korea to Thailand money exchange`
- Germany / Europe: `EUR to THB cash`, `Europe to Thailand exchange rate`

## Rollout order
1. Strengthen English landing pages and metadata
2. Expand English long-tail pages for major symbols and cash pairs
3. Add `ja`, `ko`, `de` locale shells only after the English structure is stable
4. Publish country / traveler intent pages tied to supported currencies

## Content rules
- Keep legal and methodology content indexable and accurate.
- Do not publish thin auto-translated locale pages without localized headings, metadata, and body copy.
- Keep titles specific to user intent, not internal product terms.
- Prefer pages that answer a decision question, not just pages that restate data.
